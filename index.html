Who we are

We believe good nutrition begins with better questions.

SUN-ARINU was formed on 8 June 2023, spearheaded by the Office of the Prime Minister and the Ministry of Education and Sports. It was formally launched on 17 May 2024 during the National Nutrition Forum in Kampala.

It operates as one of Uganda's official SUN Movement networks under the Uganda Nutrition Action Plan (UNAP II), alongside the SUN Business Network, SUN Civil Society Network, and Development Partners Group.

The network harnesses the collective expertise of universities, researchers, and research institutions — turning nutrition science into training, policy, and practice that reaches communities across Uganda's four regions.

The nutrition challenge

Uganda's triple burden of malnutrition.

Undernutrition, micronutrient deficiencies, and rising overweight coexist. Progress is real — but uneven. Four sub-regions still face very high child stunting: Ankole, Tooro, Kigezi, and Karamoja.

◆ Four sub-regions with the highest child stunting (≥30%) — Ankole (31.2%), Tooro (38.7%), Kigezi (41.6%), Karamoja (43.9%).

Governance

Anchored in government, run by its members.

Membership

Bring your institution on board.

Membership is open to academic and research institutions operating in Uganda that meet the network's eligibility criteria under the Uganda Nutrition Action Plan. Applications are reviewed by the Executive Council and Secretariat.

  • Academic institutions accredited by the National Council for Higher Education offering nutrition-related qualifications.
  • Research institutions approved by UNCST or registered non-profits with ethical clearance.
  • International research institutions licensed to operate in Uganda are welcome as ex-official members.
  • Institutions must align with SUN-ARINU's constitution and code of conduct.
