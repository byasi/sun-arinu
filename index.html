Aligned with

Anchored in global, continental & national frameworks.

SUN-ARINU's research is deliberately positioned to serve the agendas that shape nutrition action — from the Sustainable Development Goals down to Uganda's own development plans.

01

Global alignment

Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Our research directly drives SDG 2 (Zero Hunger) by targeting targets 2.1 (access to safe, nutritious food) and 2.2 (ending all forms of malnutrition). Furthermore, our studies intersect with SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being) and SDG 13 (Climate Action) by analysing how climate shocks alter household food security and crop nutrient density.

Nutrition for Growth (N4G) Commitments

We support global accountability frameworks by designing robust monitoring tools. This ensures that international and domestic financial pledges are optimised and directed toward high-impact, evidence-based nutrition interventions.

02

Continental alignment

AU Kampala CAADP Declaration (2026–2035)

SUN-ARINU actively services the CAADP operational cycle (Governance · Diagnostics · Investment · Accountability). We provide the localised dietary, soil, and market data required to craft National Agrifood System Investment Plans (NASIPs) and feed into the Biennial Review (BR) scorecard mechanism.

03

Country alignment

Uganda Vision 2040

Chronic undernutrition and childhood stunting impose an annual GDP drain of 5.6% due to lost cognitive and physical productivity. SUN-ARINU's work directly protects Uganda's macroeconomic future by generating interventions to lower stunting rates.

NDPIV Human Capital Development

Under the Fourth National Development Plan, Uganda aims for double-digit economic growth and the creation of 4.4 million jobs. We support the Human Capital Development Programme of NDPIV by providing the multi-sectoral data needed to close implementation gaps between agro-industrialisation and household-level dietary diversity.

04

Operational alignment

SUN Movement Strategy 4.0

Shifting from a centralised model to country-led ownership, SUN 4.0 prioritises institutional sustainability, shock resilience, and the "double burden" of malnutrition (coexisting undernutrition and obesity). SUN-ARINU serves as the localised knowledge-management anchor for this strategy.

Uganda Nutrition Action Plan (UNAP)

Coordinated by the Office of the Prime Minister (OPM), UNAP bridges the gap between Nutrition-Specific actions (clinical interventions, maternal and child supplementation) and Nutrition-Sensitive actions (biofortification, WASH, and school feeding). SUN-ARINU validates these interventions through rigorous field monitoring and evaluation.
